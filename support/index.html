Frequently Asked Questions

How is my tax calculated?
TaxPaddy calculates your tax based on Nigerian Personal Income Tax regulations, including the ₦800,000 tax-free threshold, various deductions (pension, NHF, NHIS, life insurance), and the progressive tax rate structure mandated by Nigerian tax law.
Is my data secure?
Yes! We take your privacy seriously. All your data is encrypted and stored securely using industry-standard security practices. We comply with the Nigeria Data Protection Regulation (NDPR). You can read our full Privacy Policy for more details.
What is the subscription cost?
TaxPaddy offers flexible subscription options:
• Monthly: ₦499/month
• Annual: ₦5,000/year (includes one month free)
All subscriptions include a free trial period to explore all features.
How do I cancel my subscription?
You can cancel your subscription at any time through your device's subscription settings:

For iOS: Settings → Your Name → Subscriptions → TaxPaddy → Cancel Subscription

For Android: Google Play Store → Menu → Subscriptions → TaxPaddy → Cancel Subscription
Can I export my tax data?
Yes! TaxPaddy allows you to export your tax reports as PDF documents. Simply go to the Reports section and tap the export button to generate a comprehensive PDF of your income, expenses, and tax calculations.
What is rent relief and how does it work?
Rent relief is a tax deduction allowed under Nigerian tax law. TaxPaddy automatically applies 20% of your gross income as rent relief, capped at ₦100,000 annually. The app tracks your rent relief usage throughout the year.
How do recurring entries work?
Recurring entries are automatically created in the background using server-side processing. You don't need to open the app daily - your recurring income and expenses will be added automatically based on your schedule (daily, weekly, monthly, or yearly).
Do I need to manually track receipts?
No! TaxPaddy includes built-in receipt management. You can capture receipt photos directly in the app, and they're automatically stored in the cloud and linked to your transactions.
How do loan transactions affect my taxes?
TaxPaddy properly handles loan transactions according to Nigerian tax law. Loan principal amounts are marked as tax-exempt (they're not income), while interest paid on loans can be tracked as an expense. This ensures accurate tax calculations.
What devices are supported?
TaxPaddy is available for both iOS and Android devices. The app requires iOS 13.0 or later for iPhone users, and Android 8.0 or later for Android users.

Common Issues & Solutions

Login/Authentication Issues

Problem: Can't log in or receive verification email

Solution:

  • Check your spam/junk folder for the verification email
  • Ensure you're using the correct email address
  • Try the "Forgot Password" option to reset your password
  • Clear app cache and try again
  • Contact support if the issue persists

Transactions Not Syncing

Problem: Transactions not appearing or syncing across devices

Solution:

  • Check your internet connection
  • Pull down to refresh on the transactions screen
  • Log out and log back in
  • Ensure you're logged into the same account on all devices

Incorrect Tax Calculations

Problem: Tax amount seems incorrect

Solution:

  • Verify all income entries are correct
  • Check that deductions (pension, NHF, etc.) are properly entered
  • Ensure rent relief is calculated correctly (20% cap at ₦100,000)
  • Review the tax breakdown in the Reports section
  • Contact support with specific details if calculations still seem wrong

Payment/Subscription Issues

Problem: Subscription not activating or payment failing

Solution:

  • Verify your payment method has sufficient funds
  • Check your bank or card isn't blocking the transaction
  • Ensure your device's subscription settings are correct
  • Try a different payment method
  • Contact support with your transaction ID
